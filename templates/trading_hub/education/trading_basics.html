Trading Basics

Understanding Order Types

Market Orders

A market order is an instruction to buy or sell an asset immediately at the current market price. When you place a market order, you're agreeing to pay whatever the current price is when your order executes.

Pros: Guaranteed execution, immediate transactions

Cons: No price guarantee, potential slippage in volatile markets

Limit Orders

A limit order allows you to set a specific price at which you want to buy or sell an asset. The order will only execute if the market reaches your specified price.

  • Buy Limit Order: Will only execute at the limit price or lower
  • Sell Limit Order: Will only execute at the limit price or higher

Pros: Price control, potential for better prices

Cons: No guarantee of execution if the market doesn't reach your price

Stop Orders

Stop orders become market orders when a specified price (the stop price) is reached.

  • Stop-Loss Order: Designed to limit an investor's loss on a position
  • Stop-Limit Order: Combines features of stop and limit orders

Pros: Automated risk management, pre-planned exit strategy

Cons: Can be triggered by temporary price fluctuations

Market Terminology

Bull Market
A market characterized by rising prices and optimistic investor sentiment.
Bear Market
A market characterized by falling prices and pessimistic investor sentiment.
Spread
The difference between the bid price (what buyers are willing to pay) and the ask price (what sellers are willing to accept).
Volume
The total amount of a cryptocurrency that has been traded within a specific period.
Market Cap
The total value of a cryptocurrency, calculated by multiplying the current price by the total circulating supply.
Liquidity
The ability to quickly buy or sell an asset without causing a significant change in its price.

Quick Tips for Beginners

  • Start with small amounts
  • Research before investing
  • Diversify your portfolio
  • Use limit orders to control prices
  • Keep records of all trades
  • Never invest more than you can afford to lose
