Cryptocurrency Fundamentals

What is Blockchain?

A blockchain is a distributed ledger that records transactions across many computers so that any involved record cannot be altered retroactively, without the alteration of all subsequent blocks.

Key Features of Blockchain Technology:

  • Decentralization: No single entity has control over the entire network
  • Transparency: All transactions are visible to anyone on the network
  • Immutability: Once recorded, data cannot be altered
  • Security: Cryptographic techniques secure transactions

Blockchain serves as the foundation technology for all cryptocurrencies, allowing them to function without a central authority.

Understanding Cryptocurrencies

Cryptocurrencies are digital or virtual currencies that use cryptography for security and operate on blockchain technology. Unlike traditional currencies issued by governments (fiat currencies), cryptocurrencies are typically not controlled by any central authority.

Types of Cryptocurrencies:

  • Bitcoin (BTC): The first and most well-known cryptocurrency
  • Ethereum (ETH): A platform for decentralized applications and smart contracts
  • Stablecoins: Cryptocurrencies designed to maintain a stable value, often pegged to fiat currencies
  • Altcoins: Alternative cryptocurrencies launched after Bitcoin
  • Tokens: Digital assets built on top of existing blockchain platforms

Tokenomics

Tokenomics refers to the economics of a cryptocurrency or token. It encompasses all aspects that make a particular token valuable and attractive for investors.

Key Components of Tokenomics:

  • Supply: Total, circulating, and maximum supply of tokens
  • Distribution: How tokens are allocated (team, investors, community)
  • Utility: What the token is used for within its ecosystem
  • Inflation/Deflation: Whether new tokens are created or destroyed over time
  • Governance: How token holders participate in decision-making

Understanding tokenomics is crucial for evaluating the long-term potential of a cryptocurrency investment.

Cryptocurrency Wallets

  • Hot Wallets: Connected to the internet, convenient but less secure
  • Cold Wallets: Offline storage, more secure for long-term holdings
  • Hardware Wallets: Physical devices designed to secure crypto
  • Paper Wallets: Physical documents containing keys
  • Custodial Wallets: Third party holds your crypto
